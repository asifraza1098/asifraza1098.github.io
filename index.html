About Me

I use data to answer practical questions, build statistical models, and explain results in a way that is easy to understand.

I am a Data Science and Analytics Engineering graduate student with hands-on experience in Python, R, SQL, Excel, data visualization, regression analysis, panel data methods, and applied analytics. My work includes econometric modeling, business analysis, financial data projects, and machine learning workflows.

This portfolio highlights selected academic and applied projects that show my ability to clean data, explore patterns, build models, interpret results, and communicate insights clearly.

R / Econometrics

Ordered Health Models:
Logit and Probit Analysis

Analyzed self-reported health status using ordered logit and ordered probit models in R. The project compares ordinal response models, estimates predicted probabilities, calculates average marginal effects, tests the proportional-odds assumption, and includes a robustness check with a nonlinear age term.

Tools: R, MASS, marginaleffects, brant
Skills: Ordered logit, ordered probit, ordinal regression, marginal effects, model comparison, econometric interpretation
